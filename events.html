🌿 Seasonal Brew

Spring Foraging Brew Day

10:00 AM – 4:00 PM · Brewery & Grounds

Our most popular annual event. Join head brewer Emma for a morning guided walk through the property to harvest fresh spruce tips, wild herbs, and whatever else the spring has produced. After the walk, watch us brew a one-off seasonal ale with the foraged ingredients right in front of you.

You'll learn about wild ingredient identification, the brewing process from mash to boil, and how we incorporate foraged botanicals into beer recipes. The finished beer will be released 4 weeks later at the taproom — participants get a reserved 4-pack before it goes on sale.

Wear boots — the trails are muddy in April. We'll provide baskets, gloves, and clippers for the harvest. Lunch included: smoked meat sandwiches and a pint of your choice.

$45 per person · Includes lunch + beer + reserved 4-pack · Limited to 20 spots · Book via contact page

🍺 Beer Release

Spruce Tip Saison Release Party

2:00 PM – 8:00 PM · Taproom & Patio

The most anticipated release of the year. Our Spruce Tip Saison — brewed with fresh tips harvested from the property in April — goes on tap and into cans for the first time this season. This is the beer that put Walk in the Woods on the map, and it only comes around once a year.

We'll have the saison pouring alongside a special cask-conditioned version dry-hopped with fresh spruce tips from the same harvest. Live music from local songwriter Meg Chicken from 4–7 PM. Wood-fired flatbreads and smoked meats available all afternoon.

Cans will be available for purchase at the taproom — limit 2 four-packs per person on release day. Online orders open the following Monday. Last year it sold out in 10 days.

Free admission · First 50 arrivals get a free tasting glass · Cans $6.50 each

🎓 Workshop

Intro to Homebrewing Workshop

11:00 AM – 3:00 PM · Brewhouse

Always wanted to brew your own beer but don't know where to start? Emma walks you through the entire process — from grain selection and mashing to boiling, hopping, fermentation, and bottling. You'll brew a 20-litre batch of our Morning Fog Lager recipe on a homebrewing setup and take it home to ferment.

No experience needed. We provide all equipment, ingredients, and a homebrewing starter kit to take home. You'll leave knowing enough to brew your second batch on your own. Class is capped at 8 people so everyone gets hands-on time at each stage.

Includes a full flight tasting, lunch from the kitchen, and a detailed recipe booklet with 5 of our beginner-friendly recipes adapted for homebrewing.

$95 per person · Includes all materials + homebrewing starter kit + lunch · Max 8 people

Brewmaster's Dinner: Summer Solstice

6:00 PM – 10:00 PM · Taproom (Indoor)

A five-course dinner paired with five Walk in the Woods beers, prepared by guest chef Marcus Chicken from The Red Door in Baddeck. Each course is designed to complement the beer it's paired with — from a light ceviche with the Berry Bramble Sour to a braised short rib with the Deadfall Stout.

Every Week at the Taproom

Not everything needs to be a special event. These are the things that happen every week, rain or shine, all season long. No booking required — just show up.

🎶 Live Music Saturdays

Every Saturday, 3–6 PM · May through September

Local musicians play the patio stage every Saturday afternoon. Folk, fiddle, acoustic, and the occasional sea shanty crew. Different act each week — follow us on social media for the lineup. No cover charge. Grab a pint and a seat by the stage.

🏭 Free Brewery Tours

Every Saturday, 1 PM & 3 PM · Year-round

Walk through the brewhouse, see the hopyard (in season), taste grain from the mash tun, and learn how spring water becomes beer. Tours last about 30 minutes and include a tasting of whatever's just come off the tank. No reservation needed — just show up at the bar.

🔥 Fire Pit Fridays

Every Friday, 5–8 PM · May through October

We light the fire pit as soon as the sun starts dropping. Bring a blanket if it's cool, grab a pint of Nightcap Porter, and watch the stars come out over the highlands. The kitchen stays open until 7 PM for flatbreads and smoked meats. Our favourite night of the week.

🌲 Trail Walks

Every Sunday, 11 AM · May through October

A casual 45-minute guided walk through the property trails. Learn about the trees, the hopyard, the spring, and the wildlife (we have a resident family of foxes). Ends at the taproom for a complimentary half-pint. Family-friendly. Dogs on leash welcome. Wear sturdy shoes.

Community Is Everything

We're not just a brewery — we're a gathering place. From hosting fundraisers for the Baddeck volunteer fire department to sponsoring the local youth hockey team, we believe the best thing a business can do is give back to the community that supports it. If you're running a community event and need a venue or a donation, get in touch. We say yes more than we say no.

We also partner with local farms, artisans, and food producers whenever we can. The cheese board at the taproom features gouda from Foxhill Cheese House. The honey comes from a beekeeper three roads over. The smoked meats are from a butcher in Whycocomagh. When you spend money here, it stays in Cape Breton.
